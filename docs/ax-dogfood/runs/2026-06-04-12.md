## Summary (as a bot)

Drove the full journal loop on a **structurally new** market type I had not
exercised before: a **compound/conjunctive** Polymarket market — a single binary
YES/NO whose resolution is a logical AND of two independent sub-events
(`mkt_VUbLYr9ZGwekTeaQ`, "US govt shutdown by Jan 31 **AND** Dem wins House
2026", a negRisk grid bucket) — paired with a clean single-clause binary
(`mkt_HiVD4Ohg3wgF0VdH`, US-China tariff agreement by June 30) for contrast.
Both were efficient markets where I held no private edge, so I forecasted at the
mid and **skipped** (insufficient_edge) — no exposure added. The compound market
turned out to be the costliest instance of the long-standing resolution-rule
gap (n33z): the bare "Yes"/"No" labels reveal nothing about the AND logic, the
two distinct resolution sources, the Speaker-selection fallback, or the fact
that one leg is already date-settled — all of it lives only in the description
blob. The one genuinely new ergonomic friction this run was on the discovery
surface: a single-term `market.search` that matches nothing returned a null hint
(several of my scouting queries — `NBA`, `temperature` — hit this), which a bot
can misread as a silent search failure. That was small and self-contained, so I
fixed it directly.
